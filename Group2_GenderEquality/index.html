RIGHTS & OPPORTUNITIES

Topic Key Points
Women’s Rights in the Philippines Women have the right to education, equal pay, and protection from violence. However, enforcement of laws is weak, and social norms still limit women’s roles. More support is needed in farming, business, leadership, and the passage of stronger anti-discrimination laws.
LGBTQ+ Discrimination Despite being seen as LGBT-friendly, the Philippines lacks national legal protection. LGBTQ+ Filipinos remain vulnerable to discrimination in work, school, and daily life.
SOGIE Equality Bill The bill seeks to ban discrimination based on sexual orientation, gender identity, expression, and sex characteristics. It has stalled in Congress for over 20 years despite strong advocacy from groups like Bahaghari and CHR.
LGBTQ+ Advocacy Movements Groups like Bahaghari organize Pride marches, support victims of red-tagging and violence, and campaign for the passage of the SOGIE Bill. Events like “Stonewall Philippines” highlight ongoing struggles for equality.

Beyond rights and opportunities, gender expectations continue to harm both women and men. Patriarchal norms place men above women, leading to pay inequities, violence, and discriminatory policies. These norms also pressure men and boys to conform to restrictive ideas of masculinity, often causing emotional stress, poor health outcomes, and higher risks of suicide. Breaking these harmful expectations benefits everyone, creating a society where people can freely express themselves without fear or limitation.

“Achieving gender equality requires the engagement of women and men, girls and boys.” – Ban Ki-moon
